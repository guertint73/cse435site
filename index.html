Project Info

Project Background

Parking is a very intensive and stressful procedure for a driver of a vehicle. In parallel parking maneuvers, for example, it can be difficult to see the front bumper of the car behind the one parking. Additionally, parking lots are busy areas with people getting in and out of cars and perhaps trying to park themselves. Due to the high level of activity surrounding them, drivers may be distracted while parking, which only adds to the risk involved. An automated system has the potential to be safer than a human driver in that the automated system is able to avoid distracted driving entirely. An automated system would also be able to detect objects like people walking in the vehicle's blind spots, and be able to react to stop the vehicle quicker than a human would.

Project Description

Our Active Park Assist system will allow for automatic parking, thus allowing a driver to park their vehicle smoothly and without the need for driver interaction. Though the use of ultrasonic sensors will be used to guide the vehicle into a parking spot and detect objects in the path of the car. Cameraâ€™s will identify parking spots. The system will only interact with the driver at the start and at the end of the maneuver. At the start of the procedure, the driver will verify the APA's selection of a parking spot. During the maneuver, the system will act on its own. Upon completion, the system will relinquish control back to the driver. At any time the driver may take control of the steering wheel, and initiate a manual override of the APA system.
